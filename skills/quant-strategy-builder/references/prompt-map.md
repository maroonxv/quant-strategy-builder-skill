# Prompt Map

Use these prompt starters when the user has a strategy idea but the request is still too vague to map cleanly onto code. Default to China-market framing unless the repo clearly targets another venue.

## Mean Reversion

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to turn a mean-reversion idea on liquid A-shares or CSI 300 constituents into a testable strategy with universe selection, z-score entry and exit rules, T+1-aware execution, and a backtest plan.`

## Breakout

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to design a breakout strategy for China futures with session filters, dominant-contract roll rules, stop placement, fee and slippage assumptions, and a minimal validation loop.`

## Trend Following

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to build a medium-term trend-following strategy for domestic commodity futures with moving-average or Donchian signals, pyramiding rules, roll handling, and drawdown-aware risk controls.`

## Statistical Arbitrage

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to prototype a statistical arbitrage strategy for A-share pairs or sector ETFs with pair selection, spread z-score logic, hedge ratios, rebalance cadence, and a walk-forward evaluation plan.`

## Covered Call

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to design a covered-call strategy on 50ETF or CSI 300 ETF options with underlying filters, strike and tenor rules, assignment handling, and Greeks-aware reporting.`

## Wheel

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to build a wheel strategy on exchange-listed ETF options that rotates between cash-secured puts and covered calls, including assignment transitions, capital usage, and scenario tests.`

## Iron Condor

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to structure an iron-condor strategy on China ETF options with expiration and delta selection rules, width constraints, profit targets, margin assumptions, and risk-management checks.`

## Delta-Neutral Hedging

Starter prompt:

`Use $quant-strategy-builder to create a delta-neutral China options strategy with hedge thresholds, rebalance cadence, transaction-cost assumptions, and a focused backtest plan using ETF or index-futures hedges.`
